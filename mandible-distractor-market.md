<p><p>A Mandible Distractor is a surgical device used in orthognathic procedures to gradually lengthen the mandible (lower jaw) through distraction osteogenesis. This technique involves the surgical separation of the mandible and the application of controlled tension, allowing new bone to form in the gap created. <p><ul><li>Muti-vector Distraction</li><li>Bi-directional Distraction</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The mandible distractor market consists of two main types: multi-vector distraction and bi-directional distraction. Multi-vector distraction devices allow for osteogenic movements in multiple directions, facilitating complex jaw realignment and growth. This versatility is particularly beneficial for patients needing customized corrections. In contrast, bi-directional distraction focuses on two opposing directions, enhancing jaw lengthening and alignment while minimizing surgical intervention. Both methods aim to optimize treatment outcomes in craniofacial and orthodontic surgeries by promoting effective bone regeneration and stability.</p></p>
