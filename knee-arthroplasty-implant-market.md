<p><p>The knee arthroplasty implant market is primarily divided into two types: total knee arthroplasty (TKA) implants and partial knee arthroplasty (PKA) implants. TKA implants involve replacing the entire knee joint, making them suitable for patients with severe osteoarthritis or joint degeneration. In contrast, PKA implants only replace part of the knee joint, offering a less invasive option for patients with localized damage. Both types aim to relieve pain, restore function, and improve the quality of life for individuals with knee-related issues.</p></p>
