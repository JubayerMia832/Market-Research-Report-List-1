<p><p>The food grade dextrose market comprises two primary types: dextrose monohydrate and dextrose anhydrous. Dextrose monohydrate, a crystalline form containing water, is widely used as a sweetener, texturizer, and energy source in various food products, owing to its high solubility and stability. Dextrose anhydrous, on the other hand, is the dehydrated form, characterized by its lower moisture content, making it suitable for products requiring a long shelf life and enhanced sweetness. Both types are essential in food manufacturing and processing.</p></p>
