<p><p>In the automotive market, aluminum is classified into three main types: cast, rolled, and extruded aluminum. Cast aluminum is used for intricate parts, providing strength and lightweight benefits. Rolled aluminum is processed into sheets and foils, ideal for body panels and components due to its malleability and high strength-to-weight ratio. Extruded aluminum involves shaping molten aluminum into specific profiles for applications like frames and structural supports, offering versatility and durability crucial for modern vehicle designs.</p></p>
