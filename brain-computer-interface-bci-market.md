<p><p>A Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) enables direct communication between the brain and external devices by interpreting neural signals. The market for BCIs is categorized into two main types: invasive and non-invasive. Invasive BCIs involve surgical implantation of devices that provide high-resolution neural data, suitable for applications like motor control but carry risks. Non-invasive BCIs use external sensors to capture brain activity, making them safer and more accessible, ideal for applications in gaming, rehabilitation, and mental health monitoring.</p></p>
