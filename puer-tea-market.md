<p><p>The Pu'er tea market is divided into two main types: Raw Pu'er and Cooked Pu'er. Raw Pu'er, or Sheng, undergoes minimal processing and is characterized by its fresh, grassy flavor that matures and becomes more complex with age. In contrast, Cooked Pu'er, or Shou, is fermented and aged, resulting in a smoother, earthy taste. This differentiation caters to varying consumer preferences, with enthusiasts seeking the authenticity of Raw Pu'er and new drinkers often gravitating toward the more approachable Cooked Pu'er.</p></p>
