<p><p>Deep sea mining technology encompasses three main market types: cobalt-rich crusts, manganese nodules, and seafloor massive sulfides. Cobalt-rich crusts, found on underwater mountains, are rich in critical metals for batteries and electronics. Manganese nodules, located on the ocean floor, contain valuable metals like nickel, copper, and cobalt, benefiting various industrial applications. Seafloor massive sulfides, formed at hydrothermal vents, are a source of precious metals and minerals, essential for energy and technology sectors, making these resources increasingly sought after.</p></p>
