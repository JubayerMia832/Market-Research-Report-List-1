<p><p>The major orthopedic joint replacement implants market encompasses three primary types: ceramics, metals, and polymers. Ceramic implants offer excellent wear resistance and biocompatibility, making them suitable for hip and knee replacements. Metal implants, often made from titanium or cobalt alloys, are known for their strength and durability, frequently used in load-bearing joints. Polymer implants, typically utilized in bearing surfaces, provide flexibility and cushioning, enhancing comfort. Each type plays a critical role in addressing different patient needs and surgical requirements.</p></p>
