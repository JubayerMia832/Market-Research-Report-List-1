<p><p>The 3D printing photopolymer market comprises three primary types based on the light spectrum used for curing resins. Ultraviolet (UV) photopolymers are the most common, curing rapidly with UV light, ideal for high-resolution prints. Visible light photopolymers utilize specific wavelengths of visible light, offering potential for safer operations and broader material compatibility. Infrared photopolymers, less commonly used, cure with infrared light, enabling unique material properties and applications in specialized fields. Each type caters to diverse industrial and consumer needs.</p></p>
