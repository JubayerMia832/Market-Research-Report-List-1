<p><ul><li>Halogen Molecules</li><li>Azo Compounds</li><li>Peroxides</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Radical initiators are crucial in polymerization processes and can be categorized into three main market types: halogen molecules, azo compounds, and peroxides. Halogen molecules generate radicals through thermal or photolytic processes, enabling controlled polymerization. Azo compounds decompose thermally to produce free radicals, making them widely used in plastics and coatings. Peroxides serve as efficient initiators by thermally decomposing to yield radicals, often utilized in rubber and resin applications. Each type plays a vital role in various industrial and commercial applications.</p></p>
