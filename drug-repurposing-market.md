<p><p>The drug repurposing market is categorized mainly into two types: intravenous and oral. Intravenous drug repurposing involves administering medications directly into the bloodstream, allowing for rapid action and higher bioavailability, often used in acute conditions or hospital settings. In contrast, oral drug repurposing involves administering medications through the digestive system, offering convenience and ease of use, making it suitable for chronic conditions. These distinct routes enable diverse applications and patient populations, optimizing therapeutic effectiveness and improving treatment outcomes.</p></p>
